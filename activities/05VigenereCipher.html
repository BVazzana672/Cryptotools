Cryptanalysis of the Vigenère Cipher

The Process

  1. Use the Quadgraph Search tool to find repeated quadgraphs in the ciphertext.
  2. Count the number of letters between repeated quadgraphs. You find it helpful to copy the cipher text into a Google Doc. Highlight from the beginning of one quadgraph to just before the next occurrence of it. Then under the Tools menu, select word count. The number you want is "Characters excluding spaces"
  3. Compute the GCD of the numbers you found in step 2. For best results, you will want to compute a GCD of at least three numbers, but preferably more. The length of the keyword is probably a divisor of the GCD you computed.
  4. Make a guess for the length of the keyword. Then use the Subtext Frequency Analysis tool to get a frequency analysis of each chunk of the cipher.
  5. Make a guess for the shift value for each chunk of of text from the previous step. (Remember that it's just a Caesar shift.)
  6. Use the Decryption Tool to try to decipher the text. If your guess at the key is partially correct, you should see pieces of words, and from this, you may able to deduce which letters in your guess for the keyword are correct.
